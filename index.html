Lucky A. Ventura Passed Away at 26

My Story

The literary world bids farewell to Lucky A. Ventura, a well-known author whose life story inspired generations. He passed away peacefully at the age of 26, leaving behind an etched and unforgettable impact to all hearts he touched.

Lucky A. Ventura was once a troubled teenager who strayed down a dark path. He defied the odds and turned his life upside down. After years of hardship and heart-wrenching reflection in life, he found solace in writing and started anew. His best-selling published book titled “Lucky” mirrors his roller-coaster-ride experience— from being a promising son to his parents, to a teenager who brought his life into vices and crime before ultimately finding redemption and success as a writer.

His passing remarks the end of a story, but the chapters he wrote will resume to live on. As tributes pour in from the readers and fellow writers, one message remained clear. Lucky isn't just a letter written on a paper, he is a memoir in the hearts of everyone he inspired.

Memorial Mass

Lucky A. Ventura’s fans hold a memorial mass, honoring his legacy.

Thousands of readers, aspiring writers, and long-time admirers gathered at the Epiphany of Our Lord Co-Cathedral Parish — a church in Lingayen, Lucky’s hometown — to remember his life and legacy as a renowned author whose story touched millions.

The memorial mass, organized by local authors and his loyal readers, was filled with heartfelt prayers and personal testimonies about how his works changed lives.

“His words saved me in ways I never expected.” shared by Duchess Erich Meneses, a senior high student who credits Lucky A. Ventura for giving her hope during a difficult time. “Through his words, he told us that no matter how lost we feel, there's always a way back.”

After the mass, the attendees gathered outside to light candles and release white balloons into the sky in his honor.

As the sun sets over Lingayen, the people remain, bound by the legacy of a man who turned his pain into a purpose. Though Lucky A. Ventura is gone, his words will always be remembered.
